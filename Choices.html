Online learning has taken many different forms over its history so schools and universities had a plethora of online learning formats to choose from that will better fit their way of teaching. One such format is blended learning which is a combination of online learning and in-person learning, this format gives the student more freedom around their timetable and does not limit learning hours to just the time spent in class with their teachers and fellow students. Blended learning was implemented by some courses even at AUT, the beginning of 2022 was affected by covid-19 restriction which limited the number of students allowed in the classroom at one time. Blended learning had classes that had a larger student population go online for their lectures but once the restrictions allow a large number in the classroom lab tutorials began with smaller groups in that class. Another format is fully online learning where there is no in-person learning in the classroom most schools and universities used this form at the beginning of the covid-19 lockdowns due to the heavy restrictions placed on them during these times.

Many schools were not initially prepared for fully online learning and lacked the equipment and knowledge on how to conduct their selves during this time and so were students. Many schools and universities needed to loan out equipment such as laptops and Chromebooks to their students that didnt have access to such devices. The choice that the school needed to take is whether loan out these devices with the risk of them not coming back or coming back damaged in some form or another.
